\section{Ch. 2: From Explanations to Why-Questions}

\textbf{Thesis:} Philosophers of Science have claimed to seek a theory of explanation, but what they're really after is a theory of answers to why-questions.
\newline\newline
\textbf{Support:}
\begin{itemize}
	\item Explanations and Why-questions come apart.
	\begin{itemize}
		\item ``Explains'' can take various wh-compliments (what, who, where, how), not just why.
		\item A speaker can give an answer to a why-question without \textit{explaining} it.
		\item Explanation is primartily a speech act, and as such, belongs top philosophy of language (obviously a bad pissing-contest claim)
		\item If scientists couldn't explain, they would still be able to seek answers for why-questions, and would still be good scientists\ldots though they would be bad public intellectuals.
	\end{itemize}
	\item Hempel's DN model is a theory of answers to why-questions.
	\item Because he thought he had to give a theory of explanation, Hempel had to try to get the DN model to provide explanations-what, leading him to take seriously objections which, had he focussed simply on answers to why-questions, he could have ignored.
	\item Contra Hempel, ``explains-wh'' is not obviously ambiguous. Mirrors the behavior of knows-wh, which is not ambiguous.

	
	\item A theory of explanation must have the following form: Fact F is an explanation of Fact G \textit{iff}\ldots  (p.14)
	\item Such a theory deprives us of important distinstions, such as that between ``merely'' partial answers to a why-questions and partyial answers that are also \textit{part} pf a complete answer.
	\item Hempel distinguished between \textit{explanation-seeking} why-questions and \textit{epistemic} why-questions, but this difference seems more to do with the content of what follows the ``Why'' than with the content of why-questions in general.
	\item Skow does restrict the range of why-questions he's interested in:
	\begin{itemize}
		\item He's only interested in why-questions that get ``because'' answers, not ``in order to'' answers.
		\item Not interested in Anscombean why-questions. (\jm{Doesn't this rule out many why-questions in the social sciences?})
	\end{itemize}
	
\end{itemize}
